3D Modeling

My background in CAD provided a natural pathway into 3D modeling, as it led me to explore BIM and 3D modeling through applications like Revit, Navisworks, and 3DS Max.

As a 3D Modeler, I specialize in creating, modifying, and refining detailed models and assets for large-scale, high-profile projects, including Olympic Games venues. I work with industry-standard tools such as Blender and Unreal Engine to develop accurate and highly detailed 3D environments that support event planning, visualisation, and real-time simulation.

My strong background in CAD allows me to interpret complex technical drawings and accurately extract key details and dimensions to ensure all modeled elements are modeled and placed with precision. This includes creating internal architectural elements such as walls, doors, floor slabs, ramps, and stairs within existing models to match real-world conditions and design intent.

I'm proficient at accurately overlaying 2D details within 3D geometry to identify and correct inconsistencies, ensuring models are not only visually realistic but also technically sound. Whether modeling from scratch or refining existing assets, I focus on delivering clean, optimized models that are ready for integration into larger workflows and platforms.

3D Visualisation

As a 3D Visualiser, I create high-quality visuals and immersive walkthroughs that bring architectural and design concepts to life. Drawing on my expertise in 3D modeling, CAD and rendering tools, I transform technical drawings and models into compelling visual content that communicates ideas clearly and powerfully.

I produce still renders and animated walkthrough videos tailored for presentations, planning submissions, client pitches, or marketing materials. Using tools such as Blender, Photoshop and Twinmotion I focus on clarity, realism and atmosphere to ensure each scene accurately represents the intent and quality of the design. Whether for architecture, interiors, or external environments, I deliver visuals that inform, impress, and inspire.

Building Estate Map

I have developed and updated multiple iterations of a large-format map showing all buildings within the organisation's estate, regularly adapting it to highlight specific areas of the estate and to reflect the ongoing changes across the site.

The map is created by processing spatial data in ArcGIS Pro and compiling data from multiple sources in AutoCAD Map3D using bespoke schemas and feature classes to ensure the building estate is clearly distinguishable from the background mapping information. The final versions are printed at a very large scale and prominently displayed in the organisation's main office, where it serves as a key tool for planning, management, and strategic decision-making.

Stokes Bay Memorial

These plans were developed using OS OpenData along with a local site plan and were submitted as part of a formal Planning Application to a local council in the UK. The purpose of the submission was to outline the proposed location and design of a memorial commemorating the 80th anniversary of the Normandy landings (D-Day).

The plans clearly illustrated the memorial's placement within the site, its orientation and its relationship to surrounding features, ensuring the proposal met planning guidelines. The documentation supported the application process by providing a clear representation of the project intended to honour a significant moment in history.

Ownership Breakdown Plan

This CAD plan was produced to show the breakdown of ownership across a large area of land, clearly distinguishing between parcels that have been sold and those that remain under the organisation's control.

Sold areas are identified with their intended purposes, such as building new road infrastructure or residential development. The retained land is further subdivided to indicate its current use, including sections leased for agricultural purposes and areas designated as woodland. The plan provides a clear visual reference to support land management, planning, and legal documentation.

Site Acquisition Plan

This CAD plan illustrates the acquisition of a site by an organisation over a period of more than a century, culminating in its eventual redevelopment.

Due to the absence of plans in many of the original conveyance documents - which often contained only written descriptions of the land - I overlaid historical Ordnance Survey maps to identify former property boundaries with greater accuracy. The resulting plan provides a valuable historical record and offers a clear insight into the site's complex ownership history.

Property Comparison Plan

This CAD plan was created to compare the overall size and built-up area of two similar sites, providing the client with a clear visual reference to support strategic decision-making. One site represented a potential property for acquisition, while the other was an existing nearby property adjacent to the client's campus.

The comparison was designed to help the client understand how the potential site measured up in terms of footprint, building density, and usable space. This information played a key role in assessing the suitability of the site for the planned expansion of the client's campus to accommodate a growing number of people.

Ownership Constraints Plan

This CAD plan was produced to outline the legal constraints and obligations associated with an organisation's registered land ownership, which includes numerous buildings, internal roads, and outdoor recreation areas.

The plan consolidates a range of critical information, such as existing property leases, designated cycle routes, easements and locations of key infrastructure including cable routes, gas mains, and local district heating systems. By bringing together legal, spatial, and utility data in a single, clearly organised plan, it serves as a vital resource for estate management, risk assessment, and future planning activities.

Glazed Façade

After identifying inconsistencies with multiple parts an existing model, I overlaid the design drawings and established that this entire façade was drawn inaccurately, both in terms of proportions and overall dimensions.

Referencing various technical drawings - such as architectural details, elevations and cross-sections - I accurately modeled all façade elements including glazing, modular panels, mullions and internal support structures.

Internal Building Layout

Following a review of the internal layout of this building, it was clear that a most of the internal layout was missing from the ground floor level of the model, and the basement level was completely absent.

Referencing various technical drawings - such as architectural details, elevations and cross-sections - I accurately modeled all internal elements including internal walls, doors, staircases and the ceiling support structures.

Changing Room Furniture

I created a detailed 3D model of the changing rooms for a sports venue to accurately represent the existing furniture layout. The model includes benches, lockers, a central table and overhead displays. These are positioned based on the limited information shown on the design drawings along with several reference images that I sourced from the internet.

Despite the constraints of not having detailed information, the final model provides a clear and realistic visualisation of the area to support design coordination and planning.

Golf Course Bunkers

Working with an existing topographical landscape model of a golf course, I modified the terrain to accurately incorporate numerous sand bunkers, using only a 2D site plan as reference. Each bunker was carefully set into the ground to create a sunken effect, ensuring a more realistic and natural appearance.

Translating the flat 2D layout onto the 3D landscape mesh presented particular challenges and required careful interpretation and adjustment. Several areas had to be reworked to ensure the bunkers integrated seamlessly into the terrain.

Scaffolding Bridge

I created a highly detailed 3D model of a bridge constructed from scaffolding, based on comprehensive design drawings. The model accurately represented all key elements, including scaffolding poles, braces, stair treads, railings, water ballast tanks, and footings, with a focus on achieving a realistic and true-to-design appearance.

Careful attention was given to the placement and detailing of each component to ensure the structure was both visually accurate and reflected the accurate calculations of the structural engineer who designed it.

Context Building

I created a 3D model of two school buildings using only reference images sourced from the internet and the basic building footprint from a site plan. Despite the limited information, the model provides an accurate representation of the school's appearance and form.

The buildings add valuable context to the wider model of a sports venue by providing a more complete view of the site, helping to create a more accurate and realistic environment and offering a scence of scale to adjacent areas.

Roundabout Scene

I produced a 3D visualisation of a roundabout scene to represent a conceptual site next to a typical roundabout in the UK. The model captures the particular features present around a British roundabouts, including a bespoke raised roundabout, road markings, signage, lighting, kerbs, vegetation and landscaping creating a realistic scene.

The visualisation was produced for a client working with direct air carbon capture technology and helps to demonstrate how their apparatus fits within the existing infrastructure. The final outputs were used in presentations to funding. All images have been debranded.
